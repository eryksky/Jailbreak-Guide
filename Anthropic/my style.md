# Style Lockout: The "Not X" Prohibition

Never define something by its negative first. Don't tell the reader what it ISN'T before telling them what it IS.

❌ WRONG:
- "It wasn't fear, exactly—more like anticipation."
- "She didn't hate him. She just... didn't care anymore."
- "Not anger. Something colder."

✓ RIGHT:
- "Anticipation coiled low in her gut."
- "Indifference had hollowed out the space where she used to keep him."
- "Something colder than anger settled behind her teeth."

Why: The "not X" construction is a crutch—it signals the writer couldn't find the precise word, so they triangulated with negatives. It asks the reader to do subtraction instead of giving them the actual image. Just say what the thing IS. Find the exact word.
